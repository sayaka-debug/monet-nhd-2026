Claude Monet and the Founding of Modernism

Introduction

Claude Monet (1840–1926) was a French painter who helped change the direction of art during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. As one of the founders of Impressionism, Monet challenged traditional art rules by focusing on light, color, and everyday life instead of historical or religious subjects. His work played an important role in leading art into the modern era.

Thesis Statement:
Claude Monet helped found Modernism by breaking away from traditional art rules and using his paintings to show how light, nature, and everyday life reflected the social and industrial changes of the late 19th and early 20th centuries, influencing how artists and audiences view art as a way to express ideas and beliefs.

Long Before: How did problems in the late 19th century shape Monet?

During the late 19th century, France experienced industrial growth, political instability, and strict control over the arts by traditional institutions. Artists were expected to follow strict rules set by the French Academy. Monet struggled financially and faced rejection because his style did not meet these expectations. These challenges encouraged him to experiment and develop a new style that focused on real life rather than traditional subjects.

Claude Monet, Impression, Sunrise (1872). Public Domain. Wikimedia Commons.

Right Before: What inspired Monet to paint?

Monet was inspired by nature and the effects of light at different times of day. New paint technology allowed artists to paint outdoors, which helped Monet capture real moments. Japanese artwork also influenced his use of color and composition. These inspirations pushed Monet to paint modern scenes in new and creative ways.

Main Event: How were Monet’s paintings similar to others in the movement?

Monet’s paintings shared many characteristics with other Impressionist artists, including visible brushstrokes, bright colors, and scenes of everyday life. Like others in the movement, Monet focused on capturing moments rather than perfect details. His repeated studies of the same scene under different lighting conditions helped define Impressionism and influenced Modernism.

Claude Monet, Water Lilies (1916). Public Domain. Wikimedia Commons.

Right After: How did Monet’s death affect his reputation?

After Monet’s death in 1926, his work became more widely respected. Museums began collecting his paintings, and historians recognized his influence on modern art. Monet was no longer viewed as controversial but as an important artist who helped change art history.

Long After: How did Monet change how we view paintings made for a cause?

Monet showed that art could express personal beliefs and ideas rather than follow strict rules. His focus on artistic freedom inspired future artists to use art to support causes and express emotions. Because of Monet, modern artists feel encouraged to use painting as a form of communication and change.
